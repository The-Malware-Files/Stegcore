For most users this works exactly the way it should; individuals,
researchers, security teams, journalists, schools, NGOs, and any
project willing to release its own source code can use Stegcore freely
under the AGPL with no further obligation.

For users who cannot meet AGPL's source-release terms (typically
companies integrating Stegcore into proprietary products, internal
tools or hosted services that they do not intend to open-source), a
**commercial licence is available** as an alternative.

## How dual licensing works

Every line of Stegcore is available under one of two terms:

| You can use Stegcore under | If you agree to |
|---|---|
| **AGPL-3.0-or-later** (the default) | Release your full corresponding source code, under the same licence, when you distribute or run your modified version as a service |
| **A paid commercial licence** (this document) | Use Stegcore inside proprietary or closed-source software without the source-release obligation |

You pick the licence that fits your situation. The codebase is the
same under either.

## Who typically needs the commercial licence

You probably do not need the commercial licence if you are:

- An individual using Stegcore as published
- A research group, journalism organisation, NGO, or educational
  institution
- An open-source project willing to license your derivative work
  under AGPL-3.0-or-later

You probably **do** need the commercial licence if you are:

- A corporation embedding Stegcore in a product you do not plan to
  open-source
- A company building an internal tool on top of Stegcore that you
  also do not plan to open-source
- A SaaS provider exposing Stegcore-derived functionality without
  releasing the surrounding service code under AGPL
- An OEM, reseller, integrator, or consultancy redistributing a
  modified Stegcore to clients under a proprietary or closed-source
  arrangement

If you are unsure which side of the line your use case falls on,
ask. We would rather have a five-minute conversation than have you
guess wrong.

## What a commercial licence includes

The commercial licence is negotiated per-customer; common terms are:

- Right to use, modify and redistribute Stegcore inside proprietary
  software without triggering the AGPL source-release requirements
- Right to ship Stegcore as part of a hosted or internal service
  without publishing the service's source code
- Indemnification, warranty, and support terms (priced in)
- Optional: priority bug-fix and security-patch turnaround
- Optional: custom feature engagement against your roadmap

Pricing depends on company size, scope of use, and which support
tier you need. We do not list a public price because the right
number is different for a 10-person studio than for a 5,000-person
enterprise.

## What is not in scope

The commercial licence covers your right to use Stegcore. It is not
a permit to bypass the [Acceptable Use Policy](AUP.md). The AUP
applies to every user of Stegcore regardless of which licence they
use. Buying a commercial licence does not waive any AUP obligation.

Contributions to Stegcore are accepted under the AGPL-3.0-or-later
licence of the project. By submitting a contribution you confirm
you have the right to license your contribution to The Malware
Files under those terms, and that we may relicense the project's
combined work (including your contribution) under future versions
of the AGPL or under a separate commercial licence as offered in
this document. This is the same pattern used by Qt, MySQL, and
other dual-licensed projects with strong reasons.

If you would prefer to keep a contribution outside the dual-licence
arrangement, mention it in the pull-request body and we will
discuss it before merging.
